# Experimental Psycholinguistics

## Course Goals

The goal of the course is to help you:

1. Understand the basic cognitive models of language processes
2. Understand basic concepts of statistical analysis and experimental design
3. Interpret the results of psycholinguistic experiments in the context of cognitive models of language processes
4. Learn to critically evaluate primary and secondary literature in the fields of psycholinguistics and neurolinguistics


## What does this mean in practice?
There are two components to the course: _Lectures_, and _Labs_:

### Lectures
In the lectures, we will discuss some of the current cognitive and neurobiological models of language in the brain. The goal for these lectures is to develop a general framework for understanding how language is "represented" in the brain: how the brain can hear, understand, and produce language. This is a survey course, which means that we will touch briefly on a variety of topics, but will not go into any great depth on any of them. For the lectures, you will need to read, think, and be ready to discuss. 

### Labs
In the labs, we will divide our time between learning about the basics of statistical analysis, and taking a closer look at how experiments are used to deepen our understanding of the cognitive processes that support language. The point of the labs is to sharpen the critical skills you will need to write your exam papers. For the labs, you will need to have an open mind, and put in some time every week engaging with challenging material. Sometimes this will be fighting with the statistics software, sometimes this will be struggling to understand what a _p-value_ is and why anyone should care, and sometimes it will be seeking to understand what researchers actually did in an experimental report, and what their results mean. My hope is that you will find it all worth it by the end!

 
## Exam
The exam for this course is a **short paper** on a topic of your choice. The paper can be written individually, or in a group of up to three people. The topic should be in the field of psycholinguistics or neurolinguistics. The name of the course is "Language, Cognition, and the Brain", and you paper should be about at least two of these things, and one of them has to be language! I highly reccomend you check with me about your topic before committing to it. 

Please note that there is a **prerequisite** for submitting the exam paper. Before you can submit your exam, I have to approve a short (2-4 page) report of a small psycholinguistic experiment that you have conducted, either on your own, or in a group. I strongly reccomend doing these experiments in a group! I will give you more details on how to do this report at the beginning of the semester.
